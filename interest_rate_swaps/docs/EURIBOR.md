# 💶 What Is EURIBOR?

**EURIBOR** stands for **Euro Interbank Offered Rate**. It’s a benchmark interest rate that reflects the average rate at which major European banks lend to one another **without collateral** in the euro money market.

---

## 🏦 How EURIBOR Works

- **Published Daily** by the European Money Markets Institute (EMMI)
- **Calculated From**: A panel of large, reputable eurozone banks
- **Method**:
  - Each bank submits its estimated lending rate
  - The highest and lowest 15% of quotes are discarded
  - The remaining rates are averaged and rounded to three decimal places

---

## 📆 EURIBOR Maturities

There are **five key maturities**, each with its own rate:

| Maturity     | Description             |
|--------------|--------------------------|
| 1 Week       | Very short-term lending  |
| 1 Month      | Monthly rate             |
| 3 Months     | Common for mortgages     |
| 6 Months     | Medium-term benchmark    |
| 12 Months    | Longest standard maturity|

---

## 🧭 Why EURIBOR Matters

EURIBOR is used as a reference rate for:

- 🏠 Mortgages
- 💳 Savings accounts
- 🚗 Car loans
- 📈 Interest rate swaps and futures
- 📊 Financial modeling and derivatives pricing

---

## 🔁 EURIBOR vs. LIBOR

| Feature       | EURIBOR                          | LIBOR                          |
|---------------|----------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| Region        | Eurozone                         | London (global currencies)     |
| Currencies    | Euro only                        | Multiple (USD, GBP, etc.)      |
| Admin Body    | EMMI                             | ICE Benchmark Administration   |
| Status        | Still active                     | Phased out (as of 2023)        |

---

## 📉 Influencing Factors

EURIBOR rates are shaped by:

- ECB interest rate policy
- Inflation expectations
- Eurozone economic growth
- Liquidity conditions in interbank markets
